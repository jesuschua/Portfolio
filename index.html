What I do professionally

I help bring sensible software development practices to legacy systems. Simple things like git for version control, CICD for deployment, and dashboards for test monitoring. These are relatively low-tech, quick to implement, and inexpensive. Orchestrated together, small increments make a big difference in the pace and quality of software delivery.

Areas I care most about

Process Automation

• Listening to stakeholders and validating their "as-is" states
• Branching out possible paths for improvement
• Choosing the right tools, technologies, and finding good spots where AI or self-evolving technologies can help
• Ensuring that critical use cases are covered
• Deploying safely with fallback options
• Having a healthy respect for the unknowns

Test Automation

• Answering the "Why" questions deeply enough so that the test cases write themselves
• Choosing end-to-end versus component testing
• Enabling static and dynamic test data to flow through the scenarios seamlessly
• Positioning manual checks where it matters
• Defining a "Passed" state that is understood by all. And even more importantly, "Failed" states that are clear-cut and actionable

Monitoring

• Creating truthful, clear, and beautiful presentations
• Designing dashboards that are easy to understand at a glance
• Getting the right balance between graph detail and simplicity

Documentation

• Understanding how less documentaion is better documentation
• Asking: "Who reads this and why?"
"Who updates this and when?"
"How do I know if this is correct?"
